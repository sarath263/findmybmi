BMI and Children

BMI for children is calculated differently than for adults and takes into account age and sex.

  • Underweight, Normal Weight, Overweight, Obese - These are the categories used to classify BMI in children.
  • Percentile Charts - These are used to interpret a child's BMI based on their age and sex.
  • Growth Monitoring - Regular BMI measurements help track a child's growth over time.
  • Health Risks - Understanding BMI in children can help identify potential health risks such as diabetes, heart disease, and joint problems.
  • Healthy Lifestyle - Educating children and parents about maintaining a healthy diet and regular physical activity to achieve and maintain a healthy BMI.
